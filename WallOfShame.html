Unfortunately, I wasn't warnered about this in bold, and my website host did not support TLS. The error FileZilla gave me was that my host's certificate was bad. So I went to FileZilla's website, and downloaded a new version of FileZilla. This version used SourceForge to install FileZilla. When SourceForge wanted to install additional software, I did not agree to their term. It didn't matter; it didn't opt me out. It installed software that hijacked all my browsers and made the default search Yahoo. Yeah, I tried to opt out, and so did others. See the link below:

AT&T Internet Service and Email

On July 29, 2016, I tried to help my cousin set up her email client program, Microsof Live Mail, so she could send emails. She wasn't having a problem recieving emails. I traced this problem to October 2015 when AT&T retired their old email servers and required everyone to change the names on thier clients. As AT&T requested, I changed the server names to "inbound.att.net" and outbound.att.net. This fixed her problem with sending emails, but her client program kept trying to re-read the emails that were already in her inbox.

I knew better, but my cousin called AT&T technical support. Needless to say, " English" was not the girls first language. She wasted my time "insisting" that I log into AT&T's web email, and see if I could read email there. Then she told me that this was all she could do, and she was going to transfer me to AT&T Connecteck, and there would be a fee. If AT&T is going to provide email with their Internet Service, then they should support it.
